Storm season can cause significant damage to unprepared roofs. Taking proactive steps before severe weather arrives can prevent leaks, structural damage, and costly emergency repairs. Here's your complete storm preparation checklist.

## Before Storm Season: Your Checklist

### Schedule a Professional Inspection

The best thing you can do is have a professional roofer inspect your roof before storm season. They can identify and fix vulnerable areas that might not be visible from the ground.

### Clean Your Gutters

Clogged gutters can't properly channel water away from your home. During heavy rain, overflow can damage your siding, foundation, and even cause water to back up under your roofing.

**Gutter maintenance tips:**
- Clean gutters at least twice a year
- Check for sagging or damaged sections
- Ensure downspouts are clear and directing water away from foundation
- Consider installing gutter guards

### Trim Overhanging Branches

Tree branches hanging over your roof are a storm hazard. High winds can break branches that then fall on and damage your roof. Trim any branches within 10 feet of your roofline.

### Check Your Attic

Your attic can reveal a lot about your roof's condition:
- Look for water stains or mold
- Check insulation for moisture
- Ensure vents are clear and functioning
- Verify flashing around any penetrations

### Secure Loose Flashing

Flashing around chimneys, vents, skylights, and where the roof meets walls is often the first place storms cause damage. Make sure all flashing is firmly secured and properly sealed.

## After a Storm: What to Do

1. **Safety first**: Don't go on your roof immediately after a storm
2. **Document from the ground**: Take photos of any visible damage
3. **Check your attic**: Look for new leaks or water intrusion
4. **Call your insurance**: Document damage before any repairs
5. **Contact a roofer**: Get a professional assessment

## Signs of Storm Damage to Watch For

- Missing, cracked, or curled shingles
- Dented metal flashing or vents
- Granule loss (look in gutters)
- Water stains in attic or on ceilings
- Damaged gutters or downspouts
