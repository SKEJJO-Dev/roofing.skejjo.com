Your roof is your home's first line of defense against the elements. Knowing when it needs attention can save you from costly repairs or a full replacement. Here are five critical warning signs every homeowner should know.

## 1. Missing or Damaged Shingles

If you can see shingles that are cracked, curling, or missing entirely from the ground, it's time to call a professional. Damaged shingles allow water to penetrate your roof deck, leading to leaks and structural damage.

**What to look for:**
- Shingles with curled or clipped edges
- Bare patches where shingles are missing
- Large amounts of shingle granules in your gutters

## 2. Water Stains on Ceilings or Walls

Brown or yellow water stains on your ceiling or walls are a telltale sign of a roof leak. Even if the stain seems dry, the problem likely persists during rain.

Don't ignore water stains — even minor leaks can cause mold growth, structural damage, and electrical hazards.

## 3. Sagging Roof Deck

A sagging or drooping roof is a serious structural issue that requires immediate attention. This typically indicates rot or damage to the roof deck, rafters, or other structural components.

If you notice any sagging areas on your roof, call a professional immediately — this is a safety concern.

## 4. Granules in Gutters

Asphalt shingles shed granules as they age. Finding large quantities of granules in your gutters after rain indicates that your shingles are nearing the end of their lifespan.

A significant loss of granules means your shingles have less UV protection and are more vulnerable to weather damage.

## 5. Daylight Through Roof Boards

If you can see daylight through your attic roof boards, or if light comes through during the day, you have a serious problem. Gaps in your roof allow water, pests, and cold air to enter your home.

Check your attic on a bright day — if you see any light, schedule an inspection immediately.
